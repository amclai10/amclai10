Website Evaluations

The Economist

The Economist is a global newspaper that covers geopolitics, finance, economics, and tech. They were founded in 1843 with the goal of promoting free trade.

  • Contrast
    • Every page has incredible contrast. All text is easily readable, headers stick out and make it easy to navigate the site, and font choices make sense.
  • Repetition
    • All of their pages seemingly follow the same layout and design. There is a well defined flow and format used by the Economist that they consistently follow.
  • Alignment
    • Alignment makes sense throughout the site. They have large margins on the left and right of the page and keep the articles in the centered main.
  • Proximity
    • They use screen real estate very effectively. There is no unnecessary white space and page content is kept relatively close.

Pacific Northwest X-Ray Inc.

Pacific Northwest X-Ray Inc. sells all kinds of products related to X-Rays; this includes lead glass, radiation reducing gloves, and animal immobilizers. They are located in Gresham, Oregon.

  • Contrast
    • The contrast on this site is terrible. They have made the decision to use a repeating background image that makes it difficult to read the text.
  • Repetition
    • Repetition was clearly an afterthought when designing this site. None of the pages have a similar layout and only resemble each other by using the same hideous style throughout.
  • Alignment
    • They have extremely poor alignment on all of their pages. They. Have centered paragraphs, weirdly aligned images, and have no sense of consistency.
  • Proximity
    • Proximity of information is very poorly handled. There is an abundance of white space on every site that makes information difficult to find.
